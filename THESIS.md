# Investment Thesis

## Core axioms

These are not hypotheses. They are the operating assumptions of the advisory system. Every agent decision derives from them. If you disagree with an axiom, the entire system must be rethought before proceeding.

---

### Axiom 1: All crypto tokens are options on underlying ideas

A token's market price is the market's implied probability that the underlying idea achieves adoption sufficient to sustain value. This maps directly to option pricing theory:

| Option concept | Crypto equivalent |
|---|---|
| Underlying asset | The idea (decentralized perps, global money, programmable settlement) |
| Strike price | The adoption threshold needed to sustain value |
| Option premium | Current token price |
| Implied volatility | Narrative momentum, developer activity, institutional flow |
| Expiry | Effectively open-ended, but capital rotates away from stale narratives |
| Theta decay | Opportunity cost of capital sitting in a non-performing thesis |

**Consequence:** Most tokens expire worthless. This is not a failure of the market — it is the correct functioning of an option book. You hold many small options expecting most to expire zero and a few to return 10x–100x.

---

### Axiom 2: Crypto is winner-takes-all within each category

Within any defined category (DEX, L1 smart contract platform, SoV, stablecoin), one protocol captures the overwhelming majority of value. The rest asymptotically approach zero.

**Why:** Network effects in financial infrastructure are near-absolute. Liquidity attracts liquidity. The #1 DEX has 10x the liquidity of #2, which means tighter spreads, which means more volume, which compounds.

**Implication:** Position sizing should reflect conviction about category winner — not portfolio diversification by name. Five L1 positions is not diversification, it is diluted conviction.

---

### Axiom 3: Bitcoin is a religion, not a technology investment

Bitcoin's value is not a function of technical innovation. It is a function of belief coordination — the largest, most decentralized, most battle-tested belief that digital scarcity has monetary value.

**Analogy:** Gold. You do not invest in gold because of its industrial applications. You invest because enough people believe in it as a store of value that the belief self-reinforces.

**Portfolio implication:** Bitcoin is the low-volatility anchor of any crypto portfolio. It will not 10x from here. It will also likely not go to zero. It is the "deep in-the-money" option — high intrinsic value, low premium, low gamma.

---

### Axiom 4: Ethereum is a slow corporate boat

Ethereum has the most developer activity, the most DeFi TVL, the most institutional mindshare. It also has the most bureaucratic upgrade process, the most entrenched competing interests, and the most competition from faster, cheaper L1s eating its margin.

**Position:** Ethereum is an underperformer relative to beta. In a bull market, BTC and high-conviction L1s outperform ETH. In a bear, ETH sells off more than BTC. Hold only when thesis-specific.

---

### Axiom 5: Tsunamis cannot be predicted — only positioned for

HYPE going from $24 to $77 in six months is a tsunami. You cannot predict when. You can identify the tectonic conditions: real revenue, self-sustaining tokenomics, institutional catalyst on the horizon, dominant market position.

The BPF analysis is the seismograph. It does not predict tsunamis. It identifies which fault lines have the most pressure.

**Consequence:** The research agent reads BPF reports not to predict price, but to identify which ideas have structural conditions for a non-linear event. Position sizing reflects this: options-style exposure (limited downside, asymmetric upside).

---

### Axiom 6: Most tokens trend to zero — on average

This is not bearish. This is arithmetic. In a winner-takes-all market with open-ended expiry and no forced settlement, the expected value of a randomly selected token is zero. The advisory edge is in selection, not participation.

**Risk management implication:** Never add to a losing position without re-underwriting the thesis from first principles. The market going down is not a reason to average down — it is a reason to ask whether the idea still has adoption potential.

## The option-profile framework

Each of the 10 investment profiles maps to an option book analogy:

| Profile | Option analogy | Expected behavior |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — Pure Option | Deep OTM call spread | Most expirations are zero; one position covers all losses |
| 2 — Conviction Plays | ATM calls on 2–3 names | High vol, high return if right, total loss if wrong |
| 3 — High Beta Diversified | ATM + OTM mix | Moves 2–3x the market in both directions |
| 4 — Religion + Growth | ITM call (BTC) + ATM call (growth) | Stable core, asymmetric upside kicker |
| 5 — Balanced | Covered call structure | Participates in upside, protected on downside |
| 6 — Defensive Growth | Long BTC + small call book | BTC dominates, small option exposure |
| 7 — BTC Core | Deep ITM call | High delta, low gamma, low theta |
| 8 — Capital Preservation+ | Cash-secured put | Earns yield on stables, adds BTC if price drops |
| 9 — Yield First | Stablecoin covered call | Earns stablecoin yield, tiny BTC position as lottery |
| 10 — No Directional | T-bill equivalent | No crypto directional exposure, pure stablecoin yield |

## What the advisory system does not do

- It does not predict prices.
- It does not promise returns.
- It does not execute trades (it generates recommendations for human-reviewed execution).
- It does not override a client's stated risk profile.
- It does not extrapolate past BPF performance as future guarantee.
